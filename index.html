Password tips

Build passwords that are harder to guess

Use more length

Longer passwords resist guessing better than short passwords with tiny twists.

Mix character types

Blend lowercase, uppercase, numbers, and symbols without relying on one predictable recipe.

Prefer unique passwords

Every important account deserves its own password, especially email, banking, and work tools.

Consider a passphrase

A few unrelated words with separators can be easier to remember and much harder to attack.

Common password mistakes

Patterns attackers try early

Dictionary words with swaps

Changing letters to symbols, like a to @ or o to 0, is familiar to cracking tools.

Keyboard walks

Rows such as qwerty, asdf, zxcv, and 123456 are easy to test automatically.

Repeating blocks

Sequences like aaaaaa, 111111, or abcabcabc add length without adding much protection.

Password security

One password should not unlock your whole life

Many people reuse the same password across websites, or make small changes such as adding a symbol, an exclamation mark, or a number at the end. That feels different, but attackers often try those patterns automatically.

Reuse creates a chain reaction

If one website is hacked, attackers may test the same password, and close variations of it, on email, shopping, banking, social, and work accounts. Every important website should have a unique password.

Your email is the reset key

A password is only as secure as the email account connected to it. Your main Gmail, Hotmail, Outlook, or personal email account should be treated like a private master key because it can reset many other accounts.

Protect the address too

Avoid sharing your main private email address everywhere. The fewer places that know it, the fewer places can leak it or target it.

Small changes are not enough

Changing Password2026 to Password2026! may look stronger, but it still follows a pattern. Unique passwords reduce risk more than small edits to one reused password.

Email aliases and forwarding

Use aliases to keep your private inbox quieter

A safer approach is to use email aliases or forwarding addresses. You can keep one private email address that only you know, then use different forwarding aliases when signing up to websites.

How forwarding helps

Emails sent to an alias still arrive in your real inbox, but the website never sees your real email address. That adds another layer of privacy without needing a separate inbox for every account.

Leaks are easier to contain

If a forum or website gets hacked and your alias is leaked, you can block or delete that alias and create a new one. Your real email address stays private.

Still watch for phishing

Email aliases reduce exposure, but they do not stop phishing. Still stay alert for suspicious messages, urgent requests, fake login pages, and links you were not expecting.

Privacy tools

DuckDuckGo is one practical example

Privacy-focused tools such as DuckDuckGo Browser and DuckDuckGo Email Protection can help reduce tracking and make private email aliases easier to use. They are not magic shields, but they can help lower everyday exposure when used with careful password habits.

GoblinPass

A local helper for unique passwords

GoblinPass is a local/offline password helper designed to make unique passwords easier to manage without relying on a normal online password vault. It can help users create or manage unique passwords for different websites while keeping things simple and private.

View included GoblinPass app Open live GoblinPass demo

Simple by design

The goal is practical security: fewer reused passwords, clearer habits, and less dependence on sending sensitive information through online services.

Careful wording matters

No tool should be described as unhackable. GoblinPass can help reduce risk, but users should still protect their device, keep backups in mind, and stay alert for phishing.

Optional full login details

GoblinPass can include an option to save full login information for an entry. That can expose the email address used for the account, so it is best treated as a deliberate choice rather than a default habit.

Useful with alias emails

If you use alias emails, saving the login locally can help you keep track of which alias belongs to which website. This can be useful when the information stays local and is protected carefully.

Master password

          
One remembered secret, many regenerated passwords

          

            What if you could generate a unique secure password for every website you
            use, while only needing to remember the private master password that
            belongs to you?
          

        

        

          

            
What the master password does

            

              The master password is the private phrase you type into GoblinPass to
              generate a website password. It should be something only you know,
              strong enough to resist guessing, and memorable enough that you do not
              need to write it in unsafe places.
            

          

          

            
How regeneration works

            

              GoblinPass uses a local algorithm with the website ID, your master
              password, and the selected options. If the same ID, master password,
              length, counter, and character options are used again, the same website
              password can be regenerated.
            

          

          

            
You choose the website ID

            

              You decide which ID to use for every website. The important part is
              knowing which ID belongs to which account, because that ID becomes one
              of the ingredients used to regenerate the password later.
            

          

          

            
The password is not stored

            

              The generated password does not need to be saved in a password list. It
              is simply recreated when the same inputs match. Optional vault details
              can help track IDs, aliases, hints, and login notes, but the generated
              password itself can be regenerated locally.
            

          

        

      


      

        

          
GoblinPass and LessPass

          
Different takes on stateless password generation

          

            LessPass and GoblinPass both belong to the same broad idea: generate a
            repeatable password from inputs you control instead of storing every
            generated password in a normal online vault.
          

        

        

          

            
LessPass approach

            

              LessPass describes itself as a stateless password manager. It computes
              a unique password from a site, login, master password, and options, and
              it is designed so the generated password does not need to be saved.
            

          

          

            
GoblinPass approach

            

              GoblinPass is built around a simple website ID chosen by the user. The
              ID, master password, and options regenerate the password, while the
              optional local vault can help remember which ID, alias email, counter,
              and hint belong to each website.
            

          

          

            
The practical difference

            

              LessPass asks you to remember or enter the site and login inputs.
              GoblinPass focuses on remembering a chosen website ID and can keep
              local supporting notes if the user wants that extra convenience.
            

          

          

            
Neither removes responsibility

            

              A deterministic generator depends heavily on the strength of the master
              password and the secrecy of the inputs. It can help reduce password
              reuse, but users still need to protect their device, avoid phishing,
              and change affected passwords if an account is compromised.

About Us

          
About GoblinPass

          

            At GoblinPass, we believe password security should not be complicated.
            Every day, millions of people reuse the same password across multiple
            websites, often making only tiny changes such as adding a ! or changing
            a number at the end. When one website suffers a data breach, attackers
            can often gain access to many other accounts using the same password.
          

          

            GoblinPass was created to help solve this problem. Our goal is simple:
            make it easier for everyone to use strong, unique passwords for every
            website without needing to remember hundreds of different passwords.
            GoblinPass uses a stateless password generation system, allowing you to
            generate secure passwords whenever you need them while keeping your
            master password under your control.
          

          

            We also provide tools that help you understand and improve your password
            security. Whether you are checking the strength of an existing password,
            learning about common mistakes, or exploring better ways to protect your
            accounts, GoblinPass aims to make online security easier to understand.
          

        

        

          

            
Security First

            

              Your online security is only as strong as your weakest link. Your email
              account is often the key to resetting passwords across dozens of
              websites, so it deserves especially careful protection.
            

            
    
              
  • Use a unique password for every website.
    • 
              
  • Protect your email account with an especially strong password.
    • 
              
  • Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible.
    • 
              
  • Never share your email account credentials with anyone.
    • 
              
  • Avoid simple password variations that only change a character or two.
