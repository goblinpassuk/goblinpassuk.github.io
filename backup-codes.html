Google Backup Codes Vault

Store Google backup codes locally in your browser, encrypted with a key derived from your YubiKey. No server, no account, no cloud sync.

Local only YubiKey PRF Encrypted storage

Step 1

Set up your YubiKey

This tool uses WebAuthn PRF/hmac-secret. A normal WebAuthn login is not enough, because the YubiKey must return key material for local encryption and decryption.

If Windows shows a passkey picker, choose Security key, not the saved Windows Hello or Microsoft Password Manager passkey.

Status: not set up.

Register once per browser. After that, use Sign in to unlock existing codes or prepare the encryption key.

2FA

Step 2

Manage backup codes

Sign in with your YubiKey first. If encrypted codes are already saved, they unlock automatically. You can paste new codes, encrypt them, show saved codes, or purge everything from this browser.

Encrypted locally Hover over the sealed panel to inspect the encrypted storage status. 
Encrypted backup codes will appear here after YubiKey sign-in.

Step 3

Mark used codes

After you use a Google backup code, tick it below and remove it. The remaining codes are re-encrypted locally with your YubiKey.

Sign in and show your backup codes to manage individual codes.

Ticking a code only marks it visually. It is not deleted until you press remove.

Important

This app cannot recover your backup codes if you lose the YubiKey or the browser storage is cleared. Keep a safe offline backup somewhere you control.

WebAuthn PRF support varies by browser, operating system, and hardware key. GitHub Pages over HTTPS is suitable; opening this page directly from a local file may not allow WebAuthn.

Private or incognito windows may not share the encrypted backup-code storage from your normal browser session. You can still sign in with the same YubiKey if the browser can find the key's discoverable credential, but saved entries may appear empty in private browsing.

No cryptography here protects against a fully compromised device that can read the screen or capture decrypted codes after unlock.
