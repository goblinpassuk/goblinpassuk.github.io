GoblinPass vs LessPass

A clear comparison of two stateless approaches

LessPass and GoblinPass both generate repeatable passwords from inputs you control. GoblinPass adds a different workflow around Website IDs, optional local vault metadata, and optional extra factors.

GoblinPass approach

GoblinPass is built around a simple Website ID chosen by the user. The optional local vault can help remember which ID, alias email, counter, and hint belong to each website.

LessPass approach

LessPass describes itself as a stateless password manager. It computes a unique password from a site, login, master password, and options, without needing to save the generated password.

Why GoblinPass can feel easier

For some people, remembering or recording a small ID can be simpler than repeatedly matching exact site and login strings across devices.

Neither removes responsibility

A deterministic generator depends heavily on the strength of the master password and the secrecy of enabled inputs. Users still need to avoid phishing and protect their devices.

Google Security Factor

A major advantage over ordinary stateless generators

Traditional stateless generators usually rely on inputs the user types, such as a site, login, and master password. GoblinPass can add a Google account as an optional extra factor. That means matching passwords can require the same Website ID, master password, optional Additional Secret, and the same signed-in Google account.

The app connects through Google's Identity Services API for basic sign-in only. It does not request Gmail, Drive, Calendar, or other sensitive scopes. GoblinPass uses the stable Google account subject ID, not the email address, as hidden input material for password derivation.
